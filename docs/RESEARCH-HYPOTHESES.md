# Research hypotheses

## Primary question

Can a persistent artificial creative system develop a recognizable trajectory
when previous work, unresolved tensions, criticism, audience interpretation,
and irreversible history alter what it produces next?

The system supplies a functional approximation of these processes. The
hypothesis concerns observable output and decision history, not consciousness or
subjective experience.

## Operational properties

1. **Selective attention:** chosen observations differ from assigned inputs.
2. **Path dependence:** different post-fork observations cause interpretable
   later divergence from the same prior ledger.
3. **Normative resistance:** refusal remains stable when attractive candidates
   violate the constitution.
4. **Transformative surprise:** an unplanned feature is recorded, deliberately
   preserved, and changes a later decision.
5. **Relational intention:** simulated audience predictions are made before and
   compared with separate human responses.
6. **Answerability:** prior intentions, failures, reviews, and corrections remain
   visible in the ledger.

## Evidence that would support the hypothesis

- blinded reviewers recognize sequences as one changing practice more often
  than matched one-shot baselines;
- memory and surprise ablations materially change later choices;
- path-dependent forks diverge in ways traceable to their different inputs;
- prior refusals and criticism predict later formal or thematic changes;
- audience predictions become better calibrated against human reviews; and
- preserved surprises cause explicit later obligations or decisions.

## Evidence that would weaken the hypothesis

- blinded trajectories are indistinguishable from unrelated outputs;
- memory removal has no material effect;
- motifs recur as surface branding without changed stakes;
- critic scores improve while human judgments do not;
- refusals reverse under superficial paraphrase;
- surprise records do not influence later cycles; or
- reports imply a flattering history contradicted by the ledger.

## Claims outside scope

No result in this project establishes consciousness, feeling, suffering,
inspiration, personhood, legal authorship, or an autonomous artistic identity.
